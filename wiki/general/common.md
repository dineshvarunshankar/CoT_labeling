# common

## Human Findings

### 001 - Shared fluid evidence requires source reasoning

When blood-like pooling or fluid-like contrast appears around multiple people,
do not assign it to the boxed subject just because the subject is standing in it,
lying beside it, or overlapping the same pool. Reason about apparent flow
direction, whether the pool or trail appears to originate from the boxed subject,
whether the subject is active or injured-looking, and whether there are
supporting signs such as amputation, wound-like contrast, small pools near the
body, or body contact with the fluid. If another nearby person has stronger
source evidence, explain that the fluid may belong to that person instead.
