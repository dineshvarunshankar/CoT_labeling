# sitting_supported

## Human Findings

### 001 - Supported sitting requires a seated posture plus support

A yes answer should be supported by the subject seated with the torso upright or
partly upright while the back, torso, arms, or nearby object visibly provides
support.

### 002 - Support can come from body, object, or another person

Support may be a wall, vehicle, ground-contacting arm, stretcher, another
person, or object that visibly prevents the subject from sitting independently.

### 003 - Lying or unsupported sitting rules it out

If the subject is mostly horizontal, fully ground-contacting, or upright without
visible support contact, explain why the evidence favors lying or unsupported
sitting instead.
