# amputation_arm

## Human Findings

### 001 - Arm amputation requires visible limb termination

A yes answer should be supported by a visible missing or abruptly terminated arm
region, shortened upper limb, absent forearm/hand where one should be visible,
or strong left-right arm 'asymmetry' tied to the boxed subject.

### 002 - Hidden or tucked arms are not amputation

Do not treat an arm as amputated when it is plausibly hidden under the torso,
behind clothing, behind another person, outside the crop, or folded against the
body without a visible termination point.

### 003 - Thermal amputation evidence combines shape and contrast

In thermal images, possible arm amputation is stronger when a shortened limb
silhouette or missing distal segment aligns with bright-warm or dark-cool
contrast near the apparent termination site.

## Agent Findings

### 004 - Fluid strengthens amputation only when tied to termination

For arm amputation, red fluid or wound-like coloring is strongest when it is
localized at the abrupt arm termination point. Nearby pooling alone should not
replace the need to verify a missing forearm, hand, or shortened arm segment on
the boxed subject.
