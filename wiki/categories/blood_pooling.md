# blood_pooling

## Human Findings

### 001 - Blood pooling needs a connected pool or trail

A yes answer should be supported by a visible liquid-like region near the boxed
subject with irregular spreading edges, pooling shape, or a trail that appears
spatially connected to the body or wound area.

### 002 - Nearby stains are not always blood pooling

Do not treat a dark patch, shadow, clothing item, equipment, ground texture, or
unconnected colored/thermal region as blood pooling unless its shape and
location plausibly relate to the boxed subject.

### 003 - Thermal pooling can be warm or cooling

In thermal images, suspected pooling may be brighter than ground if warm, or
darker/grey if cooling. The key evidence is organic shape plus spatial relation
to the target subject, not color alone.
