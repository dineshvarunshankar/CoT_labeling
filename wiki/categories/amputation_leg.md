# amputation_leg

## Human Findings

### 001 - Leg amputation requires visible limb termination

A yes answer should be supported by a visibly missing or abruptly terminated leg
region, shortened lower limb, absent foot/lower leg where one should be visible,
or clear left-right leg asymmetry tied to the boxed subject.

### 002 - Occluded or cropped legs are not enough

Do not treat a leg as amputated when it is plausibly hidden by the torso, ground
contact, another limb, equipment, clothing, another person, or the edge of the
image without a visible termination point.

### 003 - Thermal leg amputation needs silhouette evidence

In thermal images, possible leg amputation is stronger when the lower-body
silhouette visibly ends early or is asymmetric and the apparent termination
region has local bright-warm or dark-cool contrast.

## Agent Findings

### 004 - RGB trauma cues at the termination point

In RGB images, an abruptly terminated leg may feature visible trauma, exposed tissue, or blood (such as a distinct red patch) at the stump. This visual evidence helps confirm a true amputation rather than a limb simply bent out of view or occluded.
