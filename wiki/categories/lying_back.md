# lying_back

## Human Findings

### 001 - Lying back is a supine body orientation

A yes answer should be supported by an elongated body in ground contact with the
front of the torso, face, chest, or knees oriented upward enough to indicate the
subject is supine.

### 002 - Side or prone posture rules out lying back

If the visible torso is rotated sideways, the face/chest appears downward, or the
body is curled on one side, explain why those cues support another lying posture
rather than lying on the back.

### 003 - Crop and occlusion can make back orientation hard

When the head, torso, or legs are cropped or hidden, use visible body surfaces,
limb orientation, and ground contact to explain whether the back-facing
orientation is supported or uncertain.
