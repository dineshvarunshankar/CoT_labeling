# lying_side

## Human Findings

### 001 - Lying side shows lateral body contact

A yes answer should be supported by the subject's torso and hips rotated onto one
side, with a lateral shoulder/arm/hip region contacting the ground and the body
not clearly face-up or face-down.

### 002 - Curled or rotated posture can still be side-lying

Bent knees, one arm under the body, or a curled posture can support lying on the
side when the torso's side surface is the main ground-contact surface.

### 003 - Do not confuse partial rotation with side-lying

If the subject is mostly supine or prone with only the head or limbs turned, do
not label side-lying unless the torso and hips are visibly rotated onto the side.
