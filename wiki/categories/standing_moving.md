# standing_moving

## Human Findings

### 001 - Standing requires upright weight-bearing posture

A yes answer for standing should be supported by an upright body with legs or
feet plausibly bearing weight, not merely a vertical crop or a person being held
upright by others.

### 002 - Moving must be visible from posture or blur cues in the still image

For a still frame, moving evidence can include walking/running posture, lifted
foot, stride stance, motion blur tied to the subject, or body position that
visibly implies active movement.

### 003 - Do not confuse medic activity nearby with target movement

If another person is standing or moving near the boxed subject, do not apply that
movement to the target unless the boxed subject's own posture supports it.
