# lying_front

## Human Findings

### 001 - Lying front is a prone body orientation

A yes answer should be supported by an elongated body in ground contact with the
face, chest, abdomen, or front of the legs oriented downward toward the ground.

### 002 - Supine or side posture rules out lying front

If the face/chest appears upward, the back appears ground-facing, or the subject
is rolled onto one side, explain why those cues do not support a prone posture.

### 003 - Prone posture may be inferred from visible contact geometry

When the face is not visible, use torso direction, shoulder/hip alignment, limb
placement, and broad front-body ground contact to assess whether the subject is
lying on the front.
