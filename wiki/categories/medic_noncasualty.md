# medic_noncasualty

## Human Findings

### 001 - Medic or non-casualty is based on the boxed subject's role

A yes answer should be supported by the boxed subject actively standing,
kneeling, walking, handling equipment, attending another person, or otherwise
appearing functional rather than being the casualty under assessment.

### 002 - Do not assign another person's medic role to the boxed subject

If a medic or helper is near the target but outside the box, use that person only
as context. The answer depends on whether the boxed subject is the medic or
non-casualty.

### 003 - Treatment interaction is strong evidence

The boxed subject touching, inspecting, carrying, bandaging, or positioning
another casualty is stronger evidence for medic/non-casualty than clothing or
proximity alone.
