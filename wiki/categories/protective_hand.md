# protective_hand

## Human Findings

### 001 - Protective hand requires contact with the subject's own body

A yes answer should be supported by the boxed subject's hand placed on or
pressing against their own torso, head, limb, wound area, or painful-looking
region in a guarding posture.

### 002 - Casual hand position is not protective guarding

Do not treat a hand as protective when it is merely resting on the ground,
holding an object, supporting posture, hidden by occlusion, or touching another
person rather than the subject's own body.

### 003 - Spatial location of the hand matters

The CoT should state where the hand is relative to the body, such as over the
abdomen, chest, head, arm, or leg, and whether that contact plausibly indicates
guarding rather than balance.
